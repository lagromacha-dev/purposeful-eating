Bulalo

Category: Skin & Joints (Collagen Support – Best)

What Bulalo Is

Bulalo is a Filipino soup made by slow-simmering beef shank bones, marrow, and connective tissue until the broth becomes rich and gelatinous.

Why People Turn to Bulalo

People choose bulalo when they feel physically depleted, tired, or in need of warmth and nourishment.

Why It Supports Skin & Joints

  • Beef shank contains bones, cartilage, and marrow
  • Long simmering releases collagen into the broth
  • The broth is consumed, not discarded

When Bulalo Is Most Helpful

  • Joint stiffness
  • Physical exhaustion
  • Cold or stressful periods

How to Cook Bulalo

Ingredients

  • Beef shank (with bone and marrow)
  • Water
  • Onion
  • Corn (optional)
  • Cabbage or pechay
  • Fish sauce or salt

Steps

  1. Boil beef shank in water and skim off impurities.
  2. Lower heat and simmer for 2–3 hours until tender.
  3. Add onion and corn; simmer until cooked.
  4. Season lightly.
  5. Add vegetables last and turn off heat.

Purposeful Eating Note

Bulalo is best eaten intentionally, not frequently.
